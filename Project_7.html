A coupled mathematical and machine learning model to predict foundation's capacity under combined loads

¿What did I do?

In this project, I developed a Yield surface model for foundations subjected to multiple combinations of loads. First, the variables of interest to produce a yield surface are selected, as a second step, the sampling space is defined. Then, a Yield surface expression is formulated and finally a machine learning model for regression is fitted using TensorFlow to automatize the process of calibration of constants for different problems.

A brief explanation is presented in the next chapters!!

Development of Yield surface model

Variable selection

The variables of interest to fit a simplified model are: c, φ, δ, H/Df and Df/B. Soil parameters c and φ are selected to represent the usual resistance parameters used in every bearing capacity formula, δ is the percentage of interface resistance between soil and foundation. The last two factors were chosen to represent geometric properties and different types of foundations. The sampling space is presented in Table 1.

A brief conclusion

The use of mathematical models with machine learning techniques allows to predict the capacity of foundations under combined loads with high accuracy using an advanced expression to describe the yield surface that captures the interaction between horizontal and moment loads for different geometries.
